Skill Development

Objective

To provide quality skill training to the needful thus resulting in the better standard of living.

Problem

“Our mission is skill development. There can be no development with a satiated system” by PM Modi on Independence day.
India will have demographic dividend, with nearly 65 per cent of the country’s population i.e 700 million in the working age group, there is huge powerhouse of human potential that needs to be tapped. At the same time “there will be an incremental skill gap of 347 million by 2022 in India”. The employability of polytechnic and ITI are reducing year on year due to poor infrastructure and quality of training. . Around 33% of among the trainee offered job either dropped out within a month 48% of the trainees were dissatisfied with either the job profile or remuneration offered to them

Skilling Value chain

Mobilization -> Skill training -> Assessment and Certification -> Placement -> Post placement support

Our Approach

  • Creating Awareness: To create national wide awareness program to make vocational education and training a visible national priority.
  • Partnership: To partner with government and private skill training agencies in imparting quality training and assessment & certification, pre and post placement support.
  • Industry Involvement: Long term association with industries in placements and curriculum development to make whole skilling ecosystem sustainable.
  • Outcome: Our aim is to provide skill development training to 1 million trainees in next two years.
