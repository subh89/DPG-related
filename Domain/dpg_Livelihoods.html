Project Weave (Empowering Weavers through Skill development)

Objective

To strengthen and promote Tussar based livelihoods through effective market linkage and skill development.

Problem

Nearly 400 farmers, 80 weavers , 40 reelers and 40 grainers are dependent on the Tussar silk in the Karimnagar district of Telangana. Being one of the largest producers of Tussar silk cocoons (which serve as raw material), but it has very minimal utilization of the produce. Various factors that were resulted in the decline of post harvest sector include lack of access to capital, market linkage facility, collapse of weaver cooperatives and poor institutional support.

Our Approach

The problems associated with the Tussar sector were addressed by establishing a Cocoon bank which serves the needs of all the stake holders which include farmers, reelers, grainers and weavers. The highlights of the model are as follows:

  • Women weaver cooperative: A womenâ€™s weaver cooperative was formed and the Cocoon bank was kept under the control of this cooperative. The cocoon bank purchases cocoons from farmers and supplies to weavers on demand basis thus resulting in fair price to farmers.
  • Skill development: Weavers and reelers were provided continuous doses skill development through government, private and NGO agencies.
  • Market Linkage: Established strong market linkages with help of state handicrafts department and sericulture department.
  • Multi stakeholder approach: Being a holistic model it involved all the stakeholders in the process thus resulting in the value at all levels.

    • Impact

    The project is running since 3 years, and it resulted in increase in number of mandays from 120 to 365, increase in wages from 136/meter to 250/meter, and fair price to farmers due to elimination of the middleman. The project instilled confidence among the weaving families which can be apparent from the three fold increase in number of looms.
